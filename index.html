About Me

Welcome to my online web portfolio! My name is Angela Wilson and I am a GIS Analyst and Cartographer. I first started in GIS on accident – I was forced to take an Intro to GIS class to complete my geography major – and by the end of the class, I realized I loved making maps.

After graduation, I went on to work for the United Nations World Food Programme as a member of the GIS team. I was responsible for producing various types of maps, including food security, conflict, and logistics maps. I left the World Food Programme at the end of 2015, when I decided to go back to school to get my masters in GIS.

I am currently a student at the University of Wisconsin’s online MS GIS program. In addition to taking three classes, I also work three days a week on the GIS team of an engineering firm, Arup, where I am responsible for web mapping, data management, and infographics.

When I’m not working or studying I love to be outside; I am an avid beach volleyball player and hiker. If I’m inside I’m trying a new recipe or binging the newest season of Game of Thrones – it’s so addicting! I’d love to hear more from you, if you’re interested in contacting me, please see the contact section of this website. Thanks!
